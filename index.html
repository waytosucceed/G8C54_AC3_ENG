üêíHomonymsüêí
Homonyms are words that have the same spelling or pronunciation but have different meanings. These words may share the same form but can refer to completely unrelated concepts.
Examples of Homonyms:
"Spring": This can mean a season of the year or a coiled metal device that can compress and expand.
"Bark": It can refer to the sound a dog makes or the outer layer of a tree.

üêíHomophonesü¶ç
Homophones are words that sound the same but have different spellings and meanings. They are pronounced identically but represent different ideas or things.
Examples of Homophones:
"Pair" and "pear": "Pair" refers to a set of two, while "pear" is a type of fruit.
"Weather" and "whether": "Weather" refers to atmospheric conditions, while "whether" is used to introduce choices or alternatives.

Intensify
Meaning: To increase in degree, strength, or severity; to make something stronger or more extreme.
Examples:
"The storm began to intensify as it approached the coast, with stronger winds and heavier rain."
"She decided to intensify her workout routine by adding more weight and increasing the duration."

Tangled
Meaning: Twisted together in a confused or complicated mass; difficult to separate or untangle.
Examples:
"The old rope was tangled, making it nearly impossible to unwind without cutting it."
"She struggled to comb through her tangled hair after a day at the beach."

Clanging
Meaning: Making a loud, resonant metallic sound, often caused by the collision of metal objects.
Examples:
"The clanging of the church bells could be heard throughout the village."
"There was a loud clanging noise as the gate swung open and hit the metal post."
